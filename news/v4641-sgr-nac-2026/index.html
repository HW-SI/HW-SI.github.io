Leptons or Hadrons? What Powers V4641 Sgr at Very High Energies

9 May 2026 | NAC 2026 web companion | Compact jet modelling and source-term interpretation

Short version. Compact BHJet/HADJet fits provide jet-power and particle-injection priors for V4641 Sgr, but the literal compact particle distributions only reach GeV to tens-of-GeV energies. The observed TeV-PeV gamma-ray halo must therefore be tested with escaped high-energy tails, downstream acceleration, and large-scale propagation beyond the compact jet model itself.

V4641 Sgr Transient black-hole X-ray binary / microquasar.
6.2 kpc Distance used in this modelling note.
6.4 M_sun Black-hole mass used here.
TeV-PeV Gamma-ray halo context from HAWC, H.E.S.S., and LHAASO.

Motivation

Cosmic rays are a major non-photonic astronomical messenger. A central feature in the all-particle cosmic-ray spectrum is the knee near ~3 PeV, where either Galactic accelerators begin to run out of power or Galactic confinement becomes inefficient. The source classes that dominate around this energy remain uncertain.

Recent detections of very-high-energy gamma rays from jetted black-hole X-ray binaries have made microquasars possible Galactic PeVatron candidates. V4641 Sgr is a demanding test case because the reported gamma-ray emission is hard, extended, and reaches hundreds of TeV, while the surrounding medium appears low-density.

Question

The current project asks whether compact jet modelling can supply the particle content and energy budget required for the observed very-high-energy and ultra-high-energy gamma-ray halo.

Two semi-analytical compact jet model families are used:

  • BHJet: a leptonic compact jet baseline.
  • HADJet: a lepto-hadronic compact jet model including non-thermal protons and hadronic channels.

The compact models calibrate the central engine. They are not assumed to be the final extended halo solution.

Workflow

  1. Multi-wavelength data selection: build quasi-simultaneous radio, optical/NIR, and RXTE SEDs for 1999 and 2003.
  2. Compact jet SED fits: fit BHJet and HADJet models for each epoch.
  3. Source-term extraction: translate fit outputs into jet power, geometry, and electron/proton source terms.
  4. Propagation tests: test literal compact spectra, then escaped TeV-PeV electron/proton tails.
  5. Detector-space comparison: compare to H.E.S.S., HAWC, and LHAASO, then forecast CTA and KM3NeT.

As of 9 May 2026, the propagation and detector-space comparison remain the next stage rather than a completed fit to H.E.S.S./HAWC/LHAASO.

Data selection

Two representative activity states are used so that the model comparison spans both a powerful outburst-like case and a cleaner hard/active-state case.

Source-term interpretation

The compact model outputs are translated into source-term anchors for propagation work. The key scale mismatch is that the compact particle distributions reach GeV to tens-of-GeV energies, while the observed gamma-ray halo requires TeV-PeV parent particles.

Branch Power anchor Literal compact particle ceiling Direct TeV-PeV halo source? Propagation role
1999 BHJet 1.25e37 erg s^-1 E_e,max ~1.5 GeV No Outburst leptonic baseline
2003 BHJet 4.84e36 erg s^-1 E_e,max ~1.2 GeV No Conservative hard-state baseline
1999 HADJet 9.75e37 erg s^-1 E_e,max ~20 GeV; E_p,max ~97 GeV No High-power mixed outburst
2003 HADJet 1.54e38 erg s^-1 E_e,max ~9 GeV; E_p,max ~21 GeV No High-power hard-state alternative

Current interpretation

The leptonic BHJet fits provide a compact baseline for radio-to-X-ray emission, but a leptonic explanation of the gamma-ray halo requires an additional large-scale mechanism: escaped high-energy electron tails, fast transport, low magnetic field, or distributed acceleration.

The HADJet branches increase the available mechanical power and introduce proton content, but their literal compact proton ceilings are still far below TeV-PeV parent energies. A hadronic explanation therefore also needs an escaped or reaccelerated proton population, and the low gas-density constraint makes pure pp emission energetically demanding.

Working conclusion. V4641 Sgr points to a two-stage problem: compact jet power and particle injection first, then large-scale acceleration, escape, and propagation.
