Research Focus

My current focus is on microquasars, stellar-mass black holes with relativistic jets, using nearby sources such as V4641 Sgr as time-resolvable laboratories for jet physics.

The central questions are how particles are accelerated in jets and how jets couple to their surrounding medium, shaping the gamma-ray and neutrino emission we can detect.

Microquasar Jets

Nearby stellar-mass black-hole systems provide laboratories for testing jet acceleration and emission models.

Multi-messenger Modeling

Jet models are connected to gamma-ray and neutrino predictions through detector-space forecasts.

Jet-Medium Coupling

Interaction regions, shocks, and particle transport link source physics to the Galactic environment.

Current Project

I am building an end-to-end pipeline that connects jet emission models, jet termination and interaction regions, Galactic particle transport, and detector-space predictions.

  • BHJet/HADJet for lepto-hadronic particle populations and radiation.
  • Jet interaction regions for shocks and energy transfer into the ambient medium.
  • Particle transport after particles leave the jet.
  • CTA and KM3NeT responses for gamma-ray and neutrino detectability forecasts.

Across Scales

This program compares microquasars and supermassive black-hole systems to clarify jet acceleration and jet-environment coupling under different scales and conditions.

Older projects in dark matter searches, solar-wind MHD, and stellar spectroscopy are kept on the research page as background to the current high-energy astrophysics direction.
